BIOWAVE™ RESEARCH

Experimental Research

Exploring AI-powered HRV analysis and NeuroHaptic™ regulation for adaptive stress-response recovery and autonomic stabilization in high-pressure environments.

Study Overview

BioWave™ prototype testing explored how intelligent biometric monitoring and adaptive NeuroHaptic™ feedback may support stress recovery and autonomic regulation under controlled stress conditions.

Participants

126 participants aged 18–32 participated in controlled stress exposure simulations involving cognitive pressure, public speaking tasks, social evaluation, and timed performance challenges.

Methodology

Participants wore BioWave™ prototypes while HRV patterns, pulse recovery speed, autonomic nervous system fluctuations, and adaptive stress responses were monitored continuously.

AI Monitoring

The BioWave™ AI engine analyzed HRV instability, breathing variability, and sympathetic activation indicators to predict escalating stress states in real time.

Experimental Results

Preliminary observations demonstrated measurable improvements in autonomic recovery efficiency, stress stabilization, and predictive stress-response detection.
